For the first set of calculations, we assumed no taxes. Capital gains tax is the tax that you pay on a stock's increase in value. For example, if you buy a stock for $10 but sell it for $100 ten years later, the government taxes your stock's $90 increase. Long and short term capital gains tax rates are typically different. Short term capital gains tax rates are typically higher, which benefits long term investors and creates challenges for day traders.
