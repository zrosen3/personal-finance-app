Simple taxed investment returns

For the first set of calculations, we assumed no taxes. For this set of calculations, let's include capital gains tax. Capital gains tax is the amount that you pay on a stock's increase in value. For example, if you buy a stock for $10 but sell it for $100 ten years later, the government will take a percentage of the $90 increase in value from your stock. Note that long and short run capital gains tax rates are typically different. Short term capital gains tax rates are typically higher, which benefits long term investors and creates challenges for day traders.
