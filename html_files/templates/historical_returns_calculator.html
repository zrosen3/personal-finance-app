The goal of this calculator is to show approximate historical returns from various investment choices. This calculator relies on a financial returns dataset compiled by the NYU stern school of business.

Investment choice definitions

S&P 500 -Represents the stock prices of top 500 US stocks
3-month treasury bill -yearly return offered by the US treasury on 3 month treasury bills.
U.S. treasury bond -yields on constant maturity 10-year bonds.
BAA corporate bond -yield on Moody's AAA and BAA corporate bonds.
Real estate -to measure real estate, the source data uses Robert Shiller's CASE index, which tracks the prices of houses over time.

While you can buy bonds directly, the S&P 500 and real estate indices are meant to represent the average returns from stocks and real estate. For those looking for investments which track these averages, index funds are a popular choice.

Index funds are portfolios of stocks whose price tracks the value of an index. Real estate and S&P 500 index funds have become increasingly popular in recent years, as the aggregation mitigates risk from individual investments. Index funds typically charge an annual maintenance fee, and it is important to research fees before purchasing any specific fund.
