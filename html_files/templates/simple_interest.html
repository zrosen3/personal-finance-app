The value of the top 500 US stocks has grown at roughly 8% over the last 40 years. The goal of page is to show the benefits of investing. I am not a financial advisor!

Return is the rate at which an investment increases over time. You can buy fixed rate bonds, which have a set rate of return, or floating rate bonds, which have returns that vary based on interest rates in the wider economy.

Stocks do not have set interest rates, but often increase in value over time. A stock's selling price increasing over time is called appreciation. In addition to appreciation, stocks pay out dividends: publicly traded stocks are required to pay out a portion of their profits in dividends to shareholders.

For this first set of calculations, you can see the gains from investing over the long term. This calculation is simplified, assuming a constant interest rate over time. You can play around with the calculator above to see how different interest rates, time horizons, and initial investment amounts affect your end returns.
